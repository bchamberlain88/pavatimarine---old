GUIDE TO BUYING A USED DRIFT BOAT

When you buy a used drift boat, the first thing you probably look for is quality. After quality, the cost of a used drift boat becomes your next concern. We have put together a list of things to look for when buying a used drift boat, as well as some tips on how to get the best used drift boat for your money.

1: What's it Made Of?
First determine what material you're looking for. Fiberglass drift boats don't last nearly as long as aluminum, and they're far more vulnerable to rocks and rapids. As a result, aluminum drift boats retain a much greater resale value, because they last much longer. If you're shopping for a used drift boat and you find that aluminum is more expensive than fiberglass, don't be alarmed. The owner is parting ways with a quality boat, and they're passing that value along to you.
